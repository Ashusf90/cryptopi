📘 Bot Strategy & Operations Manual

1. Operations Guide

🚀 How to Run the Bot

  • Start: Open your SSH terminal and run ./start.sh. This launches the bot in a background tmux session and starts the web server.
  • Stop: Click the Stop Bot button in the dashboard. This gracefully ends the trading loop.
  • Kill (Force Stop): If the dashboard is unresponsive, use SSH: pkill -f run_bot.py.
  • Test Mode: Ensure the Enable Live Trading checkbox is unchecked. The bot will simulate trades with fake money in GHOST MODE sometimes called Paper Trading.
  • Go Live: Stop the bot. Check Enable Live Trading. Save Config. Click Start Bot.

🖥️ The Terminal Drawer

The new slide-out drawer displays the raw tmux console output.

  • Read-Only: Keyboard input is disabled to prevent accidental commands (like Ctrl+C) from killing the bot via the browser.
  • Copying Text: Because mouse-scrolling is enabled, standard text selection is intercepted. Hold Shift + Click & Drag to highlight text for copying.

2. Core Strategy (The "Brain")

The bot's "brain" utilizes a Hybrid Market Analysis engine. It evaluates the Global Market Mood (Bitcoin) to set dynamic risk thresholds, while simultaneously identifying the specific Local Character of each coin (Trending or Ranging) to apply the correct strategy.

  • 1. Global vs. Local Logic (The Hybrid Brain)
    The bot uses a two-tiered decision engine:
    • Global Regime (BTC): Acts as the Traffic Light. It sets the Buy/Sell Thresholds. If BTC is BULLISH, thresholds are lower (aggressive). If BEARISH, thresholds are higher (conservative).
    • Local Character (The Coin): Acts as the Driver. The bot checks the specific coin's ADX and 50-Day SMA to decide which strategy to use.
  • 2. If Coin is TRENDING (Local ADX is High):
    The bot normally uses a Momentum strategy (Buying Breakouts).
    However, it checks the Local Trend (Coin vs. 50-Day SMA) for safety:
    • Bull Trend (Price > SMA): Standard Momentum. BUY when Score ≥ Buy Threshold.
    • Bear Trend (Price < SMA): SAFETY LOCK ACTIVE.
      The bot detects a "Crash" and BLOCKS all standard buy signals to prevent catching a falling knife.
      The Only Exception (Bear Bounce): It will take a high-risk, fixed-size trade only if:
      • 1. RSI is Deeply Oversold (RSI < 25).
      • 2. RSI is Hooking Up (Current > Previous).
  • 3. If Coin is RANGING (Local ADX is Low):
    The bot uses a Mean Reversion strategy (Buying Dips, Selling Rips).
    • Bull/Neutral Regime: BUY THE DIP when Score ≤ Sell Threshold. Requires Volume confirmation.
    • Bear Regime: SELL THE RIP when Score ≥ Buy Threshold. Ignores Volume check (to exit weak rallies).
    • HARD RULE: The bot will never buy a dip in a Bear + Ranging market.

3. Safety Protocols & Overrides

1. Bear Safety Lock (The "Anti-Crash" Shield)

Scenario: Market is Bearish + Coin is Trending (High ADX).

  • Result: ⛔ HARD BLOCK
  • Reason: High ADX in a bear market usually means a violent crash. We do not catch falling knives.

2. Oversold Override (The V-Shape Catch)

Scenario: RSI drops below 25 (Extreme Panic).

  • Result: ⚠️ BYPASS SAFETY
  • Reason: Even in a crash, an RSI this low usually guarantees a "Dead Cat Bounce." The bot ignores the Bear Safety Lock to scalp the quick rebound.

3. Max Exposure Cap

Check: Is (Crypto Value / Total Balance) > Max Invested %?

  • YES: ⛔ BLOCK BUY
  • Reason: Risk management. Prevents the bot from going "All In" at the top. It forces you to keep a cash reserve (dry powder).

4. Accumulator Mode (User Enabled)


Activated by checking Accumulator Mode in the Config Panel. It is also recommended to enable Allow Scalling In to allow true Dollar Cost Averaging by accumulation.
This mode shifts the bot's goal from Swing Trading (Catching moves) to Position Building (Farming).
  • Logic Shift: If you already hold the coin and price drops below your average entry, the bot ignores standard momentum requirements.
  • The Trigger: It looks for "Dip Buy" opportunities based on your Dip Buy ATR x setting.
  • Goal: Aggressively lowers your Cost Basis (DCA) to turn a losing position into a winning one faster.
  • RISK WARNING: This increases exposure in a downtrend. Only enable this for high-conviction assets that you are willing to hold long-term.

🌡️ The Heat Gauge

The dashboard symbol cards features a dynamic temperature badge that provides instant momentum feedback.

  • Momentum Scale: The text color shifts based on the asset's RSI and Trend Strength:
    Blue (Cold/Buy) → Purple (Warm) → Red (Hot/Sell)
  • How to read it:
    • Blue: Market is cooling off or oversold. (Potential Entry)
    • Purple: Market is neutral or trending normally.
    • Red: Market is overheated or overbought. (Potential Exit)

4. Config Panel Dictionary

A complete breakdown of every setting available in the dashboard.

1. Bot Operation

  • Enable Warm-Up: If checked, the bot waits for X cycles before making new buys. Indicators need time to fill their buffers (e.g., ADX needs 14 candles).
  • Warm-Up Cycles: The number of cycles to wait (e.g., 25).
  • Trending Interval: Seconds to sleep between scans when the market is Moving (High ADX). Standard: 60s.
  • Ranging Interval: Seconds to sleep when the market is Choppy (Low ADX). Standard: 300s (5m) to save API calls.

2. Strategy Toggles & Extras

  • Scarcity Mode: Bull Market Only. If enabled, the bot ignores "Overbought" sell signals (RSI > 70) as long as momentum is high. Prevents selling too early in a parabolic run.
  • Allow Scaling In: If enabled, the bot can buy more of a coin you already own (Dollar Cost Averaging). If disabled, it only buys new coins.
  • Funding Monitor: Monitors exchange Funding Rates. Penalizes the score if the crowd is too greedy (High positive funding).
  • Vol Scoring: Adjusts the trade score based on Volume/Price divergence. Adds a "Pump Boost" or "Dump Penalty".
  • Regime Index: The symbol used to judge the "Global" market mood (Default: BTC/USD).

3. Market Analysis

  • Scan Top Movers: If enabled, ignores your manual symbol list and dynamically scans the top X coins by volume/gain.
  • Scanner Mode:
    • GAINERS: Chases coins with the highest 24h % change (High Risk).
    • VOLATILITY: Hunts for coins waking up from sleep (Smart Volatility).
  • Top N Symbols: How many coins to scan (e.g., 10 or 20).
  • ADX Period: Lookback window for trend strength (Std: 14).
  • ADX Threshold: The ADX value that separates a "Trending" market from a "Ranging" one (Std: 25).
  • Blacklist: Comma-separated list of coins to never trade (e.g., stablecoins or broken pairs).

4. Core Strategy & Thresholds

  • Neutral Buy: The base Score required to buy in a sideways market (e.g., 1.5).
  • Neutral Sell: The base Score required to sell in a sideways market (e.g., -10.0).
  • Bull/Bear Mod: A modifier applied to the thresholds based on the Global Regime.
    • In Bull markets, this is subtracted from the Buy threshold (Making it easier to buy).
    • In Bear markets, this is added (Making it harder to buy).
  • MACD Weight: How much influence the MACD momentum has on the final score (0.1 to 1.0).

5. RSI Settings

  • Overbought: RSI level (e.g., 70-85) that blocks new buys.
  • Oversold: RSI level (e.g., 30) that blocks selling (anticipating a bounce).
  • Period: Lookback length for RSI calculation (Std: 14).

6. MA Trend Filter

  • Enable Filter: If checked, the bot will Hard Block any buy if the price is below the Moving Average (MA).
  • Period: The length of the MA (e.g., 20 or 50). Keeps you on the right side of the trend.

7. Volume Confirmation

  • Use Volume Check: If checked, a buy signal is ignored unless recent volume is higher than the average.
  • Volume Timeframe: The candle size used for this check (e.g., 1h).
  • Volume Period: How many past candles to average for the "baseline" (e.g., 24).
  • Volume Multiplier: How much higher the current volume must be (e.g., 1.5x average).

8. Risk & Portfolio

  • Max Positions: Hard limit on how many different coins to hold at once.
  • Low Funds ($): Stop trading if cash drops below this amount (Reserve for gas/fees).
  • Max Invested %: CRITICAL. The % of your total account allowed in crypto. (e.g., 0.8 = 80% invested, 20% cash).
  • Dip Buy ATR x: How far price must drop below your last sell price to trigger a "Re-Buy" or Dip Buy.
  • Dip Buy Alloc %: Fixed % of cash to use when "Buying the Dip" (Overrides standard sizing).
  • Min Trade ($): The smallest trade allowed. Prevents "dust" trades.
  • Bear Safety Lock: The Anti-Crash Shield. If enabled, blocks buys when Market is Bearish + Trending.
  • Max 24h Pump: Blocks buying any coin that is already up > X% today (Anti-FOMO).

9. Profit Management

  • Use Volatility Target: If checked, calculates Take-Profit based on ATR (Dynamic).
  • Volatility Multiplier: Entry + (ATR * Multiplier). A higher multiplier targets a larger move.
  • Fixed Target %: Fallback profit target if Volatility Target is disabled.
  • Enable Vault: Activates Profit Sweeping.
  • Sweep Threshold: If Cash > (Start Capital + Threshold), the bot moves the excess profit into USDC to "Lock it away."

10. ATR Stop-Loss

  • Period: Lookback for Volatility calculation (Std: 14).
  • Timeframe: Candle size for the Stop-Loss (e.g., 1h or 4h).
  • Multiplier: High Water Mark - (ATR * Multiplier). Controls how tight the stop is.
    • Low (1.5) = Tight Stop (Safe, but frequent stops).
    • High (3.0) = Loose Stop (More room to breathe).
  • Fallback %: A fixed % stop-loss used only if ATR data is missing/broken.

11. Advanced Strategy Config

  • MTFA Weights: The "Secret Sauce." These inputs determine how much weight is given to the 1m, 1h, 6h, and 1d timeframes.
    • Trending Weights: Used when ADX is High (Follow the trend).
    • Ranging Weights: Used when ADX is Low (Fade the moves).
  • MACD Config: Advanced tuning for the MACD indicator (Fast/Slow/Signal lengths).

12. Edit Raw JSON

  • Opens a modal with the raw text of config.json.
  • Useful for copying/pasting entire configs or sharing settings with others.

13. Edit Sizing Tiers

  • Controls position sizing based on signal strength.
  • Format: [Score, Allocation].
  • Example: [2.0, 0.5] means "If Score >= 2.0, use 50% of available cash."

14. Reset Bot Data

  • Soft Reset: Wipes trade history, P&L, and resets portfolio balance to $10,000 (or your setting). Does NOT delete your config.
  • Useful for restarting a "Season" or clearing out bad test data.

15. Live Trading

  • Checkbox: The master safety switch.
  • Unchecked: Ghost Mode (Paper Trading). Real data, fake money.
  • Checked: Live Mode. Real money. Requires coinbase_keys.json.
  • Note: You must click "Save Config" and then "Start Bot" for the change to take effect.

5. Reading the Logs (The "Brain Dump")

Every time the bot scans a coin, it prints a "Decision Block." Here is how to decode it line-by-line.

[18:06:44] Analyzing DOGE/USD
-> Current Price: $0.1393 (5m: -0.03% / 24h: -0.88%)
-> Market Regime: BULL (Global) | BEAR (Local)
-> Macro Context: Struct: BEAR | Fund: 0.01%
-> Active Strategy: Trending (ADX: 31.29)
-> Volume Check: FAILED (74M <= Tgt: 434M)
-> Thresholds: BUY > 1.65 | SELL < -10.00
-> Score Calc: MTFA(0.41) + MACD(-1.85) = -1.443
-> ACTION: Idle

Line-by-Line Decode

  • Analyzing [SYMBOL]:
    The timestamp and the specific coin currently being scanned.
  • Current Price:
    The live price + recent volatility.
    (5m: -0.03%) = Short-term momentum.
    (24h: -0.88%) = Daily trend. If this is > 30%, the "Anti-Pump" lock might engage.
  • Market Regime:
    The "Traffic Light" system.
    Global (BTC): Determines if the market is safe (Bull) or dangerous (Bear).
    Local (The Coin): Determines if this specific coin is strong or weak relative to its own history.
  • Macro Context:
    Struct: The Long-Term market structure (Weekly Trend). If BEAR, "Crypto Winter" rules are active (Harder to buy).
    Fund: The Funding Rate. High positive numbers (e.g., 0.05%) mean the market is over-leveraged/greedy, which penalizes the score.
  • Active Strategy:
    Which math engine is running?
    Trending (High ADX): Looking for Breakouts.
    Ranging (Low ADX): Looking to Buy Dips / Sell Rips.
  • Volume Check:
    Is there enough money moving this coin to make it safe?
    If FAILED, the bot assumes the price move is "fake" and will block the trade.
  • Thresholds:
    The "Goal Posts." The Score Calc must be higher than BUY or lower than SELL to trigger an action. These numbers change dynamically based on market mood.
  • Score Calc:
    The final grade for the trade setup.
    MTFA: The weighted price change across timeframes (Price Direction).
    MACD: Momentum Bonus. Adds points if speeding up, subtracts if slowing down.
  • ACTION:
    The final verdict. Common statuses:
    • BUY Signal: All checks passed. Buying now.
    • Idle: No clear signal. Waiting.
    • Blocked (Bear Trend): "Safety Lock" prevented a crash buy.
    • Blocked (Max Exp): You are already fully invested.
    • Blocked (Low Vol): Price moved, but volume didn't confirm it.

7. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

🤔 Strategy & Logic

Why is the bot "IDLE" when a coin is pumping +20%?

This is usually due to ADX Lag. The bot uses a 14-day average to determine if a coin is Trending or Ranging.

If a coin has been flat for 13 days and explodes today, the mathematical average might still be low (e.g., ADX 18). The bot sees this as a "Ranging Market" and interprets the pump as a "Rip" (Selling opportunity), not a breakout. It will wait for the ADX to cross 20+ before chasing the trend.

What is the "Bear Safety Lock" and why is it blocking trades?

This is your primary defense against crashing markets. It engages when:

  • Global Regime (BTC): BEAR (Price < 50-Day SMA)
  • Local Character (Coin): TRENDING (High ADX)

A "Trending Bear" market is a Crash. The bot blocks all standard buys to prevent catching a falling knife. It will only buy if the Bear Bounce logic (RSI < 25) triggers.

Why does the Scanner blacklist "MON/USD"?

Some coins listed in the API are "Zombies"—they exist in the market list but have broken data (e.g., missing High/Low prices). If the scanner tries to calculate volatility for these without data, it can crash the cycle. We blacklist them to keep the bot running smoothly.

Why is buying blocked by "Max Exp"?

This is the Max Invested % safety setting. If you set it to 0.6 (60%), and your current crypto holdings equal 60% or more of your total account value, the bot stops buying.

It forces you to keep a cash reserve ("Dry Powder") to prevent going All-In at a market top. To fix this, either sell some positions or raise the limit in the Config panel.

💰 Money & Fees

Why is my P&L negative immediately after buying?

This is the "Fee Tax." Coinbase charges a Taker Fee (approx. 0.6%) to buy and another 0.6% to sell.

The moment you buy, you are mathematically down -1.2%. The price must rise by 1.2% just to break even. The bot's P&L display accounts for this reality so you don't have false confidence.

Why did the bot sell so quickly after buying?

This is often a Volatility Squeeze. If your Stop-Loss is too tight (e.g., 1.5% drop) and your fees are high (1.2%), the asset only needs to drop 0.3% to trigger a "Loss Prevention" exit.

Solution: Increase your atr_stop_loss_config multiplier to give the trade room to breathe.

⚙️ Technical & System

How do I backup my bot?

To preserve your full history, settings, and profiles, save these files:

  • portfolio.json (Current balance and open positions)
  • trades.db (Complete historical trade log)
  • pnl_history.csv (Data for the P&L Chart)
  • config.json (Your currently active settings)
  • config_*.json (All your Strategy Profiles like safe, sniper, etc.)
What happens if I close the web page?

Nothing! The bot continues running in the background on your Raspberry Pi (via tmux).

You can close the browser, turn off your laptop, or go on vacation. As long as the Raspberry Pi has power and internet, the bot keeps trading. To actually stop the bot, you must click the Stop Bot button in the dashboard or use the SSH terminal.

Do I need to restart the bot after changing settings?

No. The bot is designed to "Hot Reload" your settings.

When you click "Save Config", the new values are written to disk immediately. The bot will pick up these changes automatically at the start of the next trading cycle.

Note: You will see a 📝 Config Saved confirmation in the logs when this happens.
